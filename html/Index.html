Our Mission

Seedling Theatre Company is dedicated to providing an environment
where children and teens feel welcome, encouraged, and empowered
through participating in the performing arts and where instructors teach
with patience and love, striving to develop their potential in every student.

Sing!

Taught by our amazing vocal coach, Britni Favero, this group class focuses on technique as well as stage presence. Students will work on solo numbers, exploring a variety of music genres and performing in front of their class at least twice a month. An end-of-semester recital for friends and family in our theater will give our aspiring singers a chance to showcase their talents. Student confidence is built on positive peer feedback and a supportive environment. Space is limited and these classes will fill quickly.

Jungle Book

The story of the Jungle Book speaks mostly of Mowgli, an Indian boy who is raised by wolves and learns self-sufficiency and wisdom from the jungle animals. The story describes the social life of the wolf pack and, more fancifully, the justice and natural order of life in the jungle.

Lion King

The King follows the adventures of the young lion Simba, the heir of his father, Mufasa. Simba's wicked uncle, Scar, plots to usurp Mufasa's throne by luring father and son into a stampede of wildebeests. But Simba escapes, and only Mufasa is killed. Simba returns as an adult and takes back his homeland from Scar with his friends Timon and Pumbaa.

Frozen

When their kingdom becomes trapped in perpetual winter, fearless Anna joins forces with mountaineer Kristoff and his reindeer sidekick to find Anna's sister, Snow Queen Elsa, and break her icy spell.Anna and Kristoff bravely push onward in a race to save their kingdom from winter's cold grip.

Act!

Taught by Sean and Maddie, your student will be immersed in all aspects of play production including the audition process, blocking, line memorization, scene and costume transitions, script analysis and so much more. Acting skills are developed through theatre games, improv, and small group collaborative assignments. Class culminates in two performances of a full play for family and friends.
